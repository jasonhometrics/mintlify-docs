Legal issues touch every aspect of real estate. Buying, selling, renting, inheriting, investing, and resolving disputes all involve legal frameworks, rights, and protections.

This section covers consumer protections, when and how to work with attorneys, contracts and agreements, landlord-tenant relationships, ownership changes, and common property issues.

## When Legal Help Matters

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Transactions">
    Contract review, title issues, complex negotiations, and closing representation. Some states require attorney involvement in real estate transactions.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Disputes">
    Boundary conflicts, easement issues, contract disputes, landlord-tenant matters, and HOA disagreements often require legal guidance.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Ownership changes">
    Probate, trust administration, divorce settlements, and adding or removing names from title involve legal documents and procedures.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Investments">
    Entity formation, asset protection, lease drafting, and eviction procedures benefit from legal expertise.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
