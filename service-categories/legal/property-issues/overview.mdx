Understanding these issues helps owners address problems and buyers avoid surprises.

## Common Situations

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can't afford mortgage payments">
    Options include loan modification, forbearance, short sale, deed in lieu of foreclosure, or selling before foreclosure completes.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Lien discovered during sale">
    Title search reveals unpaid debts attached to property. Must be resolved before clear title can transfer.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="HOA dispute">
    Disagreements over rules, fines, architectural changes, or assessments. May require mediation or legal action.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Want to run business from home">
    Zoning laws restrict commercial activity in residential areas. May need variance or permit.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Neighbor encroaching on property">
    Fence, structure, or landscaping crosses property line. May require survey, negotiation, or legal action.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Buying investment property">
    LLC ownership provides liability protection but creates financing and tax complications.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Impact on Buying and Selling

**For buyers:**

- Title search reveals liens, easements, and encumbrances
- HOA documents show rules, fees, and financial health
- Zoning affects permitted uses
- Environmental issues may require remediation
- Disputes may delay or complicate closing

**For sellers:**

- Liens must be cleared for clean title transfer
- HOA violations may need resolution
- Disclosure requirements cover known issues
- Unresolved disputes can block sales
- Foreclosure or short sale affects credit and future buying

<Note>
  Most property issues can be resolved with proper handling. The key is identifying problems early and understanding available options.
</Note>
