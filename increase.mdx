Visibility on Hometrics reflects confidence. The platform surfaces businesses that demonstrate consistent service quality, reliable reputation signals, and clear, complete information. Visibility is earned, not assigned.

---

## Signals That Influence Visibility

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Review quality">
    Strong, consistent feedback increases trust. High ratings matter, but consistency over time matters more. A single negative review doesn't disqualify a business — repeated patterns do.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Review momentum">
    Regular activity matters more than short-term bursts. Consistent monthly reviews signal reliability, while large spikes followed by long gaps signal inconsistency.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Profile completeness">
    Clear information improves engagement and comparison. Complete profiles with accurate business details, photos, links, and services convert better and signal professionalism.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Badges">
    Badges reinforce confidence and increase interaction. Businesses with badges demonstrate sustained performance and earn higher trust from customers.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Responsiveness">
    Timely follow-through reduces friction for customers. Fast response times to inquiries signal prioritization of customer engagement.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

---

## What Improves Visibility Over Time

The actions that improve visibility are the same actions that build trust with customers:

- Maintain consistent review activity
- Keep profile information current
- Select accurate differentiators
- Respond promptly to inquiries
- Avoid unnatural review behavior

None of these require payment. All require consistent execution.

---

## What Reduces Visibility

Actions that reduce visibility signal inconsistency or low engagement:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Long gaps in activity">
    Businesses with months between reviews appear inactive or inconsistent. Regular activity signals ongoing operations and customer engagement.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Incomplete profiles">
    Missing information creates uncertainty. Customers skip profiles that lack basic details like photos, services, or contact methods.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Misleading differentiators">
    Selecting differentiators you can't support creates negative reviews when expectations aren't met. Accuracy matters more than quantity.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Inconsistent follow-through">
    Slow response times or ignored inquiries signal low prioritization of customer engagement.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Non-compliant review practices">
    Purchased reviews, employee reviews, or other manipulative practices trigger monitoring and can result in badge removal or profile suspension.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Manual vs Automated Execution

The difference between manual and automated execution is consistency.

### Manual Execution

| What it requires | Why it's difficult |
| :--- | :--- |
| Remember each step | Easy to forget during busy periods |
| Depends on workload and availability | Breaks down under pressure |
| Varies in consistency | Results fluctuate based on discipline |

### Automated Execution

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Standardized timing" icon="clock">
    Actions occur on schedule without manual intervention
  </Card>
  <Card title="Maintained cadence" icon="chart-line">
    Consistent execution regardless of workload
  </Card>
  <Card title="Reduced follow-through risk" icon="shield-check">
    Nothing depends on remembering or prioritizing manually
  </Card>
  <Card title="Predictable results" icon="chart-mixed">
    Review activity becomes more reliable and measurable
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

Automation doesn't guarantee outcomes — it reduces variability.
