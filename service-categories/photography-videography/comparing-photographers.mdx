Choosing a real estate photographer affects the visual quality of property marketing and, potentially, sale outcomes. Comparing multiple photographers helps identify the best combination of quality, service, and value.

Portfolio quality is the most important evaluation factor.

## What to Look For

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Portfolio quality">
    The most critical evaluation factor:
    
    **Technical quality:**
    - Proper exposure and lighting
    - Sharp, clear images
    - Straight, level compositions
    - Good color and white balance
    - Professional editing
    - Consistent quality across images
    
    **Compositional skill:**
    - Appealing angles and perspectives
    - Good use of space
    - Attention to detail
    - Rooms shown attractively
    - Key features highlighted
    
    Review recent work, not just best examples.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Experience">
    Relevant background:
    
    - Years shooting real estate
    - Number of properties photographed
    - Experience with similar property types
    - Experience with similar price ranges
    - Full-time vs part-time
    
    Specialized real estate experience matters more than general photography background.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Equipment and technology">
    Professional tools and capabilities:
    
    - Professional camera and lenses
    - Wide-angle lenses for interiors
    - Lighting equipment
    - Drone equipment (if offering aerial)
    - Backup equipment available
    - Current technology and software
    
    Equipment quality affects results, but skill matters more than gear.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Services offered">
    Range of capabilities:
    
    - Still photography
    - Video production
    - Drone photography and video
    - Virtual tours (3D/Matterport)
    - Floor plans
    - Twilight photography
    - Editing services
    
    One-stop shopping for multiple services can be convenient.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Turnaround time">
    Speed of delivery:
    
    - Standard turnaround time
    - Consistency meeting deadlines
    - Rush service availability
    - Typical delivery schedule
    
    Fast, reliable delivery keeps listing on schedule.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Reviews and reputation">
    What clients say:
    
    - Online reviews (Google, Yelp)
    - Agent testimonials
    - Repeat client relationships
    - Professional reputation
    - Responsiveness to feedback
    
    Consistent positive feedback indicates reliable service.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Questions to Ask

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="About their experience">
    - How long have you been photographing real estate?
    - How many properties do you photograph per month?
    - Do you specialize in any particular property type?
    - Do you work full-time as a real estate photographer?
    - Can you share recent examples of similar properties?
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="About services and packages">
    - What services do you offer?
    - What is included in each package?
    - How many edited photos will I receive?
    - What is the difference between your packages?
    - Do you offer video or drone services?
    - Can I add services Ã  la carte?
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="About the process">
    - What preparation do you recommend?
    - How long will the shoot take?
    - Should I be present during the shoot?
    - What is your standard turnaround time?
    - Do you offer rush delivery?
    - How will I receive the photos?
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="About pricing">
    - What do you charge for properties like mine?
    - What does that include?
    - Are there any additional fees?
    - Do you charge a travel fee?
    - What is your cancellation policy?
    - Do you require a deposit?
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="About quality and revisions">
    - What editing do you perform on the photos?
    - Are minor revisions included?
    - What if I'm not satisfied with the photos?
    - Can you provide examples of before and after editing?
    - How do you ensure consistent quality?
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Technical questions">
    - What equipment do you use?
    - Do you have backup equipment?
    - Are you FAA Part 107 certified? (for drone)
    - Are you insured?
    - What photo formats do you provide?
    - What are the usage rights for the photos?
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Evaluating Portfolios

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What to look for">
    When reviewing photographer's work:
    
    **Quality indicators:**
    - Rooms look spacious and inviting
    - Natural-looking lighting
    - Straight lines and level horizons
    - Clean, uncluttered compositions
    - Good color and contrast
    - Sharp focus throughout
    - Professional editing without over-processing
    
    **Red flags:**
    - Dark or poorly lit interiors
    - Blown-out windows
    - Crooked or tilted shots
    - Cluttered or unflattering angles
    - Unnatural colors
    - Over-edited or unrealistic appearance
    - Inconsistent quality
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Judging style">
    Photography style considerations:
    
    - Bright and airy vs dramatic
    - Natural vs highly stylized
    - Warm vs cool tones
    - Amount of editing visible
    
    Style preference is personal, but photos should accurately represent property while looking appealing.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Assessing range">
    Variety in portfolio:
    
    - Different property types
    - Various lighting conditions
    - Challenging spaces handled well
    - Consistency across different properties
    
    Diverse, consistently high-quality portfolio demonstrates adaptability.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Comparing Multiple Photographers

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Get quotes from 3+ photographers">
    Benefits of multiple quotes:
    
    - Understand market rates
    - Compare service offerings
    - Evaluate different styles
    - Assess communication quality
    - Negotiate better pricing
    
    Interviewing multiple photographers helps identify best fit.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Create comparison framework">
    Factors to evaluate:
    
    - Portfolio quality (most important)
    - Services included in package
    - Pricing and value
    - Turnaround time
    - Communication responsiveness
    - Experience and reviews
    - Professional presentation
    - Equipment and capabilities
    
    Weight factors based on your priorities.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Beyond lowest price">
    Don't choose on price alone:
    
    - Quality varies significantly
    - Cheap photography can hurt marketing
    - Mid-range often offers best value
    - Premium pricing should reflect expertise
    
    View photography as marketing investment, not expense.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Red Flags

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Quality concerns">
    Warning signs:
    
    - Inconsistent portfolio quality
    - Amateur-looking photos
    - Limited or no portfolio available
    - Can't show recent work
    - Over-edited or unrealistic images
    - Poor technical execution
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Professionalism issues">
    Concerning behaviors:
    
    - Poor communication or slow responses
    - Vague answers about services
    - No clear pricing or contract
    - Unreliable scheduling
    - Defensive about questions
    - No insurance or proper licensing (for drone)
    - Bad reviews or reputation
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Business practices">
    Red flags:
    
    - Requires payment before seeing results
    - Won't provide references
    - Unclear about usage rights
    - Hidden fees not disclosed
    - No cancellation policy
    - Pressure tactics
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Making the Decision

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Portfolio is primary">
    Let work quality guide decision:
    
    - Review portfolio carefully
    - Look at recent, similar properties
    - Compare quality across photographers
    - Trust your eye for appeal
    
    A photographer's work speaks for itself.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Consider reliability">
    Dependability matters:
    
    - Responsive communication
    - Clear, professional process
    - Meets deadlines consistently
    - Positive reviews on reliability
    
    Photographer delays can delay listing.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Evaluate value">
    Balance cost and quality:
    
    - Best portfolio at reasonable price
    - Services included
    - Turnaround time
    - Professional experience
    - Communication quality
    
    Slightly higher cost for significantly better quality is worthwhile investment.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Working with Your Photographer

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Clear communication">
    Set expectations:
    
    - Discuss specific needs and concerns
    - Identify features to highlight
    - Clarify any special requests
    - Confirm timing and logistics
    - Ask questions before shoot
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Proper preparation">
    Your responsibility:
    
    - Prepare property thoroughly
    - Be ready at scheduled time
    - Provide access as needed
    - Follow preparation guidance
    - Clear space of clutter
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Provide feedback">
    After receiving photos:
    
    - Review promptly
    - Communicate any concerns
    - Request revisions if needed
    - Provide positive feedback for good work
    - Leave review if satisfied
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Tip>
**Photographer selection checklist:**

- [ ] Review portfolios from at least 3 photographers
- [ ] Check reviews and reputation
- [ ] Verify appropriate insurance and licensing
- [ ] Compare services and packages
- [ ] Get written quotes
- [ ] Confirm turnaround time
- [ ] Ask about revision policy
- [ ] Review usage rights and licensing
- [ ] Assess communication quality
- [ ] Choose based on portfolio quality and value, not just price
</Tip>
