Interior design services range from one-time consultations to comprehensive project management. Understanding service levels helps clients choose the right fit for their needs, budget, and desired involvement.

Most designers offer multiple service options or customize packages.

## Full-Service Design

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What's included">
    Comprehensive design from concept to completion:
    
    **Planning phase:**
    - Initial consultation and discovery
    - Space measurement and documentation
    - Concept development
    - Space planning and layouts
    - Design presentations
    
    **Selection phase:**
    - Furniture selection
    - Material and finish specification
    - Color palette development
    - Lighting design
    - Artwork and accessory selection
    
    **Implementation phase:**
    - Procurement and ordering
    - Contractor coordination
    - Delivery scheduling
    - Installation oversight
    - Final styling
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Best for">
    - Major renovations
    - New construction
    - Whole home projects
    - Clients wanting hands-off experience
    - Complex projects requiring coordination
    - Those who value professional guidance throughout
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Client involvement">
    Clients participate in:
    
    - Initial discovery conversations
    - Design presentations and approvals
    - Selection decisions
    - Budget discussions
    
    Designer handles research, sourcing, coordination, and implementation.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Design Consultation

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What's included">
    One-time or limited consultation:
    
    - In-home meeting (1-3 hours typical)
    - Assessment of space and needs
    - Verbal or written recommendations
    - Direction on colors, furniture, layout
    - Shopping suggestions
    - Resource referrals
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Best for">
    - DIY-oriented clients
    - Those wanting professional input but not full service
    - Specific questions or challenges
    - Budget-conscious projects
    - Clients who enjoy shopping and implementing
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="What you get">
    After consultation, you receive guidance to implement yourself:
    
    - Layout suggestions
    - Color recommendations
    - Furniture style direction
    - Shopping list or resource guide
    - Written summary (varies by designer)
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Design-Only Services

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What's included">
    Designer creates complete design plan; client implements:
    
    - Full design concept and presentation
    - Floor plans and layouts
    - Furniture and finish selections
    - Shopping lists with specific items
    - Specifications for contractors
    
    Client handles ordering, delivery, contractor management.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Best for">
    - Clients comfortable managing vendors
    - Those wanting to save on implementation costs
    - Projects without complex contractor coordination
    - Budget-conscious approach to professional design
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Considerations">
    - Requires client time and effort
    - Results depend on implementation quality
    - No professional oversight during installation
    - May encounter challenges without designer support
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## E-Design (Online Design)

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What's included">
    Remote design services conducted online:
    
    - Virtual consultation
    - Client-submitted photos and measurements
    - Digital design boards and layouts
    - Online shopping lists with links
    - Email or video communication
    
    Designer never visits property in person.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Best for">
    - Budget-conscious clients
    - Simpler projects (single rooms, furnishing)
    - Clients comfortable with online communication
    - Those in areas without local designer options
    - Straightforward spaces without complex needs
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Limitations">
    - No in-person assessment
    - Relies on client-provided information
    - Less personalized than in-person service
    - Not ideal for complex renovations
    - Limited contractor coordination
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Hourly Services

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What's included">
    Designer available on hourly basis for:
    
    - Shopping assistance
    - On-site consultations
    - Design decisions and advice
    - Contractor meetings
    - Specific questions or challenges
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Best for">
    - Clients who need occasional guidance
    - Specific challenges or decisions
    - Those implementing designs themselves
    - Supplementing DIY efforts
    - Projects with unclear scope
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Considerations">
    - Costs can add up for lengthy projects
    - May lack cohesive project management
    - Good for flexibility, less for complex projects
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Specialty Services

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Kitchen and bath design">
    Specialized expertise in these complex spaces:
    
    - Layout optimization
    - Cabinetry design
    - Appliance and fixture selection
    - Material specification
    - Contractor coordination
    
    May require specialized certifications (NKBA, AKBD).
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Color consultation">
    Focused specifically on color selection:
    
    - Paint colors throughout home
    - Coordination with existing elements
    - Exterior color schemes
    - Color psychology and preferences
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Lighting design">
    Specialized focus on lighting:
    
    - Lighting plans and layouts
    - Fixture selection
    - Control systems
    - Natural light optimization
    - Task, ambient, and accent lighting
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Universal design">
    Design for accessibility and aging-in-place:
    
    - Accessibility modifications
    - Safety improvements
    - Aging-in-place planning
    - ADA compliance
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Sustainable design">
    Environmentally focused design:
    
    - Eco-friendly material selection
    - Energy efficiency
    - Indoor air quality
    - Sustainable furnishings
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Choosing the Right Service Level

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Questions to consider">
    - How much time can I dedicate to this project?
    - How confident am I making design decisions?
    - How complex is my project?
    - What is my budget for design services?
    - Do I enjoy the shopping and implementation process?
    - How important is professional coordination?
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Service level comparison">
    | Service | Cost | Your Involvement | Best For |
    |---------|------|------------------|----------|
    | Full-service | Highest | Lowest | Complex projects, hands-off |
    | Design-only | Moderate | Moderate | Comfortable implementing |
    | Consultation | Low | Highest | DIY with guidance |
    | E-design | Low-moderate | Moderate | Simple projects, remote |
    | Hourly | Variable | Variable | Specific needs |
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
